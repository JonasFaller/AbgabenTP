Chris Priestley

He was born in 1958 and is an award-winning British children's book author and illustrator. He lives, in Cambridge. Chris Priestley grew up in Wales and Gibraltar. With only nine years he won his first medal in a local newspaper’s story-writing competition. In the year 1976 he left to study illustration at Manchester. After that he worked as an illustrator wide range of clients and his work appeared regularly in The Times, The Listener and The Observer. In 2000 he published his first children's book Dog Magic. The German translation of Uncle Montague’s Tales of Terror was shortlisted for a Deutscher Jugendliteraturpreis in 2011. His book Mister Creecher won the BASH (Book Award St Helens) in 2012.

Content

It's 1939 and Harry Woods is a Spitfire pilot in the RAF. When his friend Lenny loses his leg in a dogfight with the Luftwaffe, Harry is determined to fight on. That is, until his plane is hit and he finds himself tumbling through the air high above the English Channel.

×

The main Character is Harry Woods. Even as a child, he always had the dream to fly an airplane one day. When Harry turns 18 he joins the army to become a fighter pilot. His education lasted one year. After this year he began to fly for the army. His mission was to observe the coastal of Britain and France. In the year 1943 Harry and his fellows got the Message that the Germans Luftwaffe are in front of the borders of Britain. Now their mission was to defend the borders. In this fight his best friend Lenny lost his leg. He had no chance to help him. A couple of time also his airplane gets hit. After that he was tumbling with his parachute in the air in the middle of the fight.

Harry Woods is an 18 year old boy who joined the military to become part of the RAF(royal air force). Harry is very a respected person. He fly a spitfire fighter plane. Harry has a mom, a dad and a sister named Edith. His best friend is called Lenny. He is also a RAF pilot.

Rating of the book

I would rate this book a six out of ten and recommend this book for ages 14-18.

×

Overall, I would rate this book a six out of ten because the book kept you wondering what was going to happen next. Some parts of the book got boring, because there wasn´t much going on. Another reason I gave the book a 6 out of 10 is because a lot of the time the scene would just all of a sudden change and new characters that were never introduced before. They just came into the story which made the story hard to follow. I would recommend this book for ages 14-18.
